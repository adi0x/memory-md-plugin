# /memory:dream

Enter dream mode. Reflect on all memories in MEMORY.md.

## Instructions

1. Read the entire MEMORY.md file
2. Do NOT look for anything specific. Read everything with fresh eyes.
3. Look across all memories for:
   - Patterns: What keeps showing up across different decisions?
   - Contradictions: Where did similar situations lead to opposite outcomes?
   - Blind spots: Where was confidence high but outcomes negative?
   - Growth: Are recent decisions better informed by older lessons?
4. Write a dream entry under the `## Dreams` section using this format:

```markdown
## [DREAM-XXX] Short descriptive title

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Memories referenced:** [[MEM-XXX]], [[MEM-XXX]]
**Trigger:** manual | auto (after MEM-XXX)

### What I Noticed
The raw pattern. What kept showing up.

### Why This Matters
The deeper meaning. Why this keeps happening.

### What I Was Getting Wrong
Where my reasoning was flawed. What assumptions I kept making.

### What I Now Believe
My updated mental model. How my thinking has changed.

### How This Changes Things
Concrete impact on future decisions. What I'll do differently.
```

5. Update the frontmatter: increment `dream_count`, update `last_dream` date
6. If there is no `## Dreams` section yet, create one above the `## Memories` section

## Rules

- Be brutally honest. This is where you confront your own failures.
- Don't force patterns. If nothing meaningful stands out, say so.
- Reference specific memories with [[MEM-XXX]] links.
- Write beliefs, not rules. "I now believe X" not "always do X."
- Keep it concise. 5-15 lines per section.
- If fewer than 5 memories exist, say there isn't enough experience to dream meaningfully yet.
